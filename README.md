## Operating Code

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://media.giphy.com/media/26BRzozg4TCBXv6QU/giphy.gif" width="520"/>
</p>

I’ve always admired the idea behind Batman —  
not the costume, but the mindset.

No superpowers.  
Just preparation, discipline, and an uncomfortable amount of thinking ahead.

That shows up in how I work:
- I spend disproportionate time understanding **what could go wrong**
- I’m skeptical of shiny features without clear impact
- I prefer quiet reliability over loud launches
- I design for edge cases before they become incidents

## How I Approach Product Problems

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://media.giphy.com/media/l0MYEqEzwMWFCg8rm/giphy.gif" width="500"/>
</p>

When I pick up a problem, my default flow is:
- Clarify the **real user pain** (not the symptom)
- Map constraints early — tech, ops, business, time
- Make trade-offs explicit so nothing fails silently
- Define success in metrics, not opinions

I think in **flows, dependencies, and failure modes**.  
Good products don’t just work when everything goes right —  
they behave predictably when things go wrong.
